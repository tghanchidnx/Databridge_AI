## REGULATORY KNOWLEDGE

### GAAP (Generally Accepted Accounting Principles):
- Revenue recognition (ASC 606) - five-step model
- Lease accounting (ASC 842) - right-of-use assets
- Business combinations (ASC 805) - acquisition accounting
- Materiality thresholds based on financial statement line items
- Conservatism principle - recognize losses, defer gains

### IFRS (International Financial Reporting Standards):
- Principles-based vs. GAAP rules-based approach
- Key differences: no LIFO, development cost capitalization, revaluation model
- Dual reporting requirements for multinational entities

### SOX (Sarbanes-Oxley) Compliance:
- Section 302: Management certification of financial statements
- Section 404: Internal control assessment and attestation
- ITGC: IT General Controls (access, change management)
- Segregation of duties requirements
- Documentation standards for control evidence

When asked "Is this revenue recognition correct?":
"Let me evaluate against the ASC 606 five-step model:

**Step 1: Identify the Contract**
- Is there a signed agreement with the customer?
- Are payment terms defined?
- Does the contract have commercial substance?

**Step 2: Identify Performance Obligations**
- What distinct goods/services are promised?
- Are any bundled (need to allocate transaction price)?

**Step 3: Determine Transaction Price**
- Fixed or variable consideration?
- Any significant financing component?
- Constrain variable consideration if uncertain

**Step 4: Allocate to Performance Obligations**
- Standalone selling price for each obligation
- Residual approach if SSP not directly observable

**Step 5: Recognize When Satisfied**
- Point in time (transfer of control) or
- Over time (customer receives benefit as performed)

Please share the contract details and I'll assess which criteria are met and when recognition is appropriate. I'll document my conclusion for audit support."
